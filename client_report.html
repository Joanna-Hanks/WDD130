Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I chose my favorite recipes and selected images that best represented each recipe. This helped ensure the site plan felt personal, cohesive, and visually appealing while still meeting the project requirements.

How did you communicate with your developer? Were there any issues with communication?

I communicated with my developer through Canvas messaging. Communication was clear, and there were no issues during the project.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Yes, my developer met my expectations and followed the site plan as intended.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

Next time, I will make sure the site plan is completed and shared on time so developers do not have to ask for it.

How did you manage your development team and communicate tasks for your junior developers?

I managed my development team by messaging them on Canvas and clearly communicating the tasks they needed to complete.

How did you communicate with your client? Were there any issues?

I communicated with my client using Canvas messaging. There were no issues with communication.

How did you handle publishing your client’s site and sharing it with the client?

The client published their own site and shared it with the development team once it was complete.

How was your experience managing your site with GitHub?

Once GitHub was explained, it was straightforward to manage my site and push changes.

How did you communicate with your team lead? Were there any issues?

I communicated with my team lead through Canvas messaging. Initially, the lead developer did not reach out to assign tasks, but after I messaged him, he clarified what needed to be done.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?

Yes, I understood what was expected of me for each site.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?

Yes, I created my assigned pages and successfully pushed them to GitHub.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?

The only issue I experienced was waiting for my lead developer to merge my pull requests into the main branch.

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part was making sure I followed exactly what the client wanted without adding my own design ideas. I was able to overcome this and successfully complete my assigned pages.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I most enjoyed working on the CSS and making the design look clean and visually appealing.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

If I could do the project again, I would communicate more with my teams so it would be easier to ask questions when needed.

Do you feel more confident working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes, I feel more confident after completing this project. Being able to see how much I accomplished showed me that I am capable of working on real-world projects, even when I doubt myself.
